Everything You need to know about

Formula 1 (F1) is the highest class of international racing for single-seater cars, governed by the FIA. Each season features top teams like Mercedes, Red Bull, Ferrari, and McLaren competing in a series of Grand Prix races held around the world. Every team has two drivers who race to score points for both the Drivers’ Championship and the Constructors’ Championship. Legendary drivers include Lewis Hamilton, Max Verstappen, Sebastian Vettel, and Fernando Alonso, known for their incredible speed, skill, and rivalry. The teams constantly innovate with advanced aerodynamics and engineering to gain even the slightest edge. F1 is not just about racing — it’s a mix of technology, strategy, teamwork, and raw talent, making it one of the most thrilling sports in the world. F1 weekends typically span three days: practice sessions on Friday, qualifying on Saturday, and the main race on Sunday. Qualifying decides the starting grid, with the fastest driver earning the pole position. Races are held on famous circuits like Monaco, Silverstone, and Monza, each with its own unique challenges. Pit stops, tire strategies, and weather conditions play a major role in the race outcome. The thrill of overtaking, high-speed corners, and split-second decisions make every race exciting to watch. With millions of fans worldwide, Formula 1 has grown into not just a sport but a global spectacle of speed, innovation, and passion.
